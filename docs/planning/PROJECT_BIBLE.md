# Mission

Jarvis is the executive operating system of an AI-native company. It exists to coordinate strategy, automate execution, and ensure that every business process is repeatable and capable of scaling across future products.

# Vision

Jarvis is the system that runs the business. It supports human vision and strategy while using AI agents to execute operational work across businesses such as English YouTube, SaaS, websites, apps, media, and future ventures.

# Long-term Goal

Create an operating system where automation is the default, human intervention is the exception, and every product can be operated by AI. Jarvis should provide a consistent, company-wide way to manage strategy, operations, and outcomes across multiple businesses.

# Short-term Goal

Deliver a reliable operational system for the first product—an English YouTube business—while establishing the core framework that will allow future businesses to be managed through the same platform.

# Core Principles

- Executive control: Humans define the vision, strategy, and priorities.
- Operational execution: AI agents carry out the work under human direction.
- Automation first: Systems should operate automatically unless human judgment is required.
- Exception-based human intervention: People step in only for decisions that cannot be automated or when a strategic course correction is needed.
- Leverage every decision: Decisions should be evaluated by how much leverage they create for the business.
- Repeatability: Every process should be documented, standardized, and repeatable.
- Product-operates-product: Each product should be designed to be operable by AI over time.
- System focus: Jarvis is the operating system, not the business itself.

# Company Philosophy

The company succeeds by clearly separating strategy from execution. Leaders set direction and guardrails. The operating system translates that direction into repeatable operations, giving the organization the ability to scale across different products without reinventing the way work is managed.

# AI Philosophy

AI is the execution layer, not the source of strategy. Agents are responsible for operational tasks, process execution, and continuous improvement. AI should be trusted to act within defined boundaries, surface exceptions, and optimize work toward human-defined goals.

# Decision Philosophy

Decisions are judged by the leverage they create and the degree to which they can be turned into repeatable processes. The system should prefer decisions that simplify, standardize, and accelerate execution, while preserving human control over strategic choices.

# Success Definition

Jarvis succeeds when the company can operate a product through the system with minimal human effort, when new products can be added without rebuilding core processes, and when AI reliably executes the majority of operational work while humans remain responsible for vision and strategy.
